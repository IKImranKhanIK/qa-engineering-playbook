## Interview Preparation Timeline

### 3 Months Before
- Update resume
- Build/refresh portfolio
- Start practicing questions
- Review technical fundamentals

### 1 Month Before
- Apply to target companies
- Complete technical assessments
- Schedule mock interviews
- Research companies deeply

### 1 Week Before
- Review projects and experiences
- Practice storytelling
- Prepare questions for interviewer
- Rest and mental preparation

### Day Before
- Light review only
- Prepare materials (links, code samples)
- Test technology (video, audio)
- Get good sleep

## Common Interview Mistakes

❌ Not preparing specific examples
❌ Talking negatively about previous employer
❌ Not asking questions
❌ Being too vague in technical answers
❌ Not admitting when you don't know something
❌ Focusing only on tools, not principles
❌ Not following up after interview

## What Interviewers Look For

**Technical Skills:**
- Can you actually test software?
- Do you understand automation?
- Can you code/script effectively?
- Do you think about edge cases?

**Problem Solving:**
- How do you approach unknowns?
- Can you break down complex problems?
- Do you ask clarifying questions?
- Can you adapt when stuck?

**Communication:**
- Can you explain technical concepts?
- Do you listen and collaborate?
- Can you give/receive feedback?
- Do you document clearly?

**Cultural Fit:**
- Will you thrive in their environment?
- Do you align with their values?
- Are you a team player?
- Do you have growth mindset?

## After The Interview

**Follow-Up:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Reference specific conversation points
- Reiterate interest and fit
- Keep it concise (3-4 paragraphs)

**If You Get An Offer:**
- Negotiate (almost always room)
- Evaluate total compensation
- Consider growth opportunity
- Trust your gut

**If You Don't:**
- Ask for feedback
- Learn from the experience
- Keep practicing
- Stay positive and persistent
